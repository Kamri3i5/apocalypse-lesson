Vocabulary

Key Vocabulary Toolkit

Devastating (adj.)

Causing huge amounts of destruction and shock.

"The earthquake was completely devastating."

Wipeout (n.)

Total destruction of a place, species, or society.

"A global war could lead to the complete wipeout of humanity."

Unstoppable (adj.)

Impossible to stop or prevent from happening.

"The rise of artificial intelligence seems unstoppable."

Forecast (n.)

A prediction about what will happen in the future.

"The weather forecast warns of global temperature increases."

Catastrophic (adj.)

Causing terrible damage, suffering, and destruction.

"A catastrophic disaster hit the coastal area last night."

Social Impact (n.)

The effects of a disaster on society and global markets.

"We must analyze the full social impact of this global change."

Advanced Phrases

Survival Idioms & Collocations

The point of no return

The time when you must continue with what you have decided to do, because it is no longer possible to change your mind.

"Climate change may soon reach the point of no return."

Against all odds

Despite very low chances of success.

"Against all odds, the small group survived the catastrophic winter."

A silver bullet

A simple and seemingly magical solution to a complicated problem.

"Technology is not a silver bullet for all of our ecological disasters."

A ticking time bomb

A situation or condition that is eventually going to have terrible consequences.

"The global dependency on fossil fuels is a ticking time bomb."

Matter of life and death

A very serious situation in which people could die.

"Building the bunker in time is literally a matter of life and death for the colony."

Scenario 01 · Human-Made

Nuclear War

The danger of a global nuclear war is still real today. It is not just a movies plot — it is a serious risk that many countries consider in their military plans.

  • Huge explosions and fires destroying major cities.
  • A chain reaction that breaks down global trade and food supplies.
  • Smoke blocking the sun, causing worldwide starvation and extreme cold.
Scenario 02 · From Space

Asteroid Impact

Objects from space are a constant danger to our world. They are often hard to see, and they do not care about human progress or technology.

  • An impact that releases more energy than all nuclear weapons combined.
  • Giant tsunamis, massive wildfires, and thick clouds of dust blocking the sky.
  • Total breakdown of the Earth's environment within days.
Scenario 03 · Environment

Climate Crisis

We are causing permanent damage to our planet. This is a slow disaster that is getting faster and faster every year.

  • Oceans becoming more acidic and the widespread death of coral reefs.
  • Melting ice near the poles releasing huge amounts of harmful greenhouse gases.
  • A mass extinction of animals and plants happening all around the world.
Grammar Focus

Useful Grammar Structures

Ellipsis & Substitution
Avoiding repetition by leaving out expected words or using placeholders like 'so', 'do', or 'one'.
"Will humanity survive? We hope so. If we don't [survive], the outcome will be tragic."
Advanced Infinitives
Using perfect, continuous, and passive infinitives to express complex times or ongoing actions.
"The asteroid is believed to have caused the mass extinction. We need to be acting now."
Grammar Tip
Use perfect infinitives (to have + V3) to talk about actions completed before the main verb. Use ellipsis to make your spoken English sound more natural and fluent.

Context 1: Emergency Briefing

General: "Will the defense systems hold?"

Scientist: "We hope [that they will hold]. If they don't [hold], we are lost."

Stakeholder Showdown

The Optimistic Scientist

The Pragmatic Politician

The Panic-Driven Citizen
